About Me

I am a PhD student in Geography at the University of New Mexico, specializing in spatial analysis, remote sensing, and urban sustainability. My research focuses on leveraging geospatial technologies to analyze urban heat islands, environmental monitoring, and transportation planning.

Previously, I worked as a researcher at the Carinthia University of Applied Sciences, where I contributed to building footprint digitization projects in Austria. I also conducted research at the University of New Mexico, utilizing remote sensing technology to analyze urban heat islands across various cities.

My professional experience includes serving as a spatial data analyst at Dynamic Kariz System Design and Architecture Company in Tehran, Iran, where I was involved in developing national geolocation and navigation systems. Additionally, I worked as an urban development engineer at Naghshine Mandegar Engineering Office, overseeing construction projects.

My technical expertise includes Google Earth Engine, Python, JavaScript, PostgreSQL, Node.js, and Leaflet, as well as GIS platforms such as ArcGIS. I specialize in geospatial data processing, remote sensing analysis, and web-based mapping applications.

I hold an MSc in Spatial Information Management from Carinthia University of Applied Sciences, an MSc in Urban Design from Yazd University, and a BSc in Urban Planning from University of Kurdistan.
